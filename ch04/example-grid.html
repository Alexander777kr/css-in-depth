Running tips

Cadence

Check your stride turnover. Your most efficient runners take about 180 steps per minute.

Strength

Strength training is an important part of injury prevention. Focus on your core— especially your abs and glutes.

Change it up

Don't run the same every time you hit the road. Vary your pace, and vary the distance of your runs.

Count your Z's

Lack of sleep can be correlated with a higher rate of injury. Make sure you are getting enough.

Focus on form

Run tall but relaxed. Your feet should hit the ground beneath your hips, not out in front of you.

Easy does it

Most of your runs should be taken at an easy pace.
